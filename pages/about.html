About quickler

quickler exists for businesses where the work happens first and the paperwork still drags on afterwards.

In plain terms: people do the visit, inspection, service job, or internal process, and then someone still has to type it up, tidy it up, route it, or rebuild it later. quickler is about reducing that waste without making the process feel less trustworthy.

quickler story

Built around one repeated problem

The business is centred on one repeated pattern: useful information is already there, but someone still has to spend time turning it into the finished report, record, or workflow outcome. quickler is being built to reduce that second pass.

Founder

Philip Ross

Philip Ross founded quickler in Scotland. He studied Electrical and Mechanical Engineering at the University of Strathclyde and later worked at Dijuno, an AI analytics startup, building experience in software, automation, and workflow design. That engineering and workflow background is what pushed quickler toward a serious reporting product rather than a vague software pitch.

Skills and builds

Built from real operational problems

The direction comes from real work involving reporting, structured documents, operational tracking, data cleanup, and messy handoffs. It is meant to solve practical business friction, not just look clever in a demo.

Why now

Site Reports first, Custom second

The main focus of the site is Site Reports. Custom is there for businesses that can see the fit, like the way of working, and want help with a broader internal workflow problem as well.

Current stage

Early pilot, deliberate scope

quickler is still early. It is not pretending to be a giant finished platform. The current goal is to make one repeated workflow genuinely better, in a way a sensible customer can understand and trust.

Trust

Shipped workflow systems already

Before Site Reports became the main product focus, earlier projects removed repeated admin and workflow friction for charities, publishers, and commercial teams. That matters because it shows quickler can build useful software around real working processes, not just talk about them.

Offer

Who should actually enquire

A good fit is a business that can point to one repeated report or one recurring workflow headache, explain why it wastes time now, and show what “better” should look like in everyday terms.
